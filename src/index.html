This is Seattle

Home of The Space Needle

We are home to one of the most famous Landmarks in the World. The Space Needle opened in spring of 1962 at the worlds fair. It took around 400 days to build The Space Needle.The needle at the top and thin waist like shape was inspired by an abstract sculpture by artist Don Lemon titled, “the Feminine One.” To respect Don Lemon's contribution, this sculpture is displayed outside of The Space Needle itself. 1.3 million guests visit The Space Needle per year, and nearly 60 million visitors have visited the tower since 1962. The cost to build the Space Needle in 1962 was $4.5 million. In 2018, the Century Project renovation cost of $100 million, making the total worth of The Space Needle coming to 104.5 million dollar landmark. The Space Needle is privately owned by the Wright family and sits on a 120-foot by 120-foot private parcel of land on the Seattle Center campus. The Space Needle is 605 feet tall, it offers 360-degree views from its three main viewing areas – an indoor observation deck and open-air viewing area located at 520 feet, and an observation level featuring a revolving glass floor located 500 feet above ground. It previously had a resturant that was turned into the observation level with the rotating glass floors. The Space Needle installed The Loupe, the world’s first revolving glass floor, in 2018. The Loupe offers never-seen-before views of the Space Needle’s unique architecture, elevators, and the city below. The Space Needle was designated as an official landmark in 1999. It takes 43 seconds to travel in a Space Needle elevator from the ground to the tower’s top level, 520 feet above Seattle.
